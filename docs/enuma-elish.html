Enûma Eliš

  1. When the heaven above was not named,
    𒂊𒉡𒈠e𒉡ma 𒂊𒇺elish 𒆷la 𒈾𒁍𒌋na𒁍u sha𒈠𒈬sha𒈠mu
  2. the earth below was not called by name,
    shap𒇺shaplish 𒄠𒈠𒌈am𒈠tum 𒋗𒈠shuma 𒆷la 𒍠ratzakrat
  3. but Apsu, the primeval, their progenitor,
    𒍪𒀊𒌝𒈠apsu𒌝ma rish𒌅𒌋rish𒌅u 𒍝𒊒𒋗𒌦za𒊒𒋗un
  4. Mummu and Tiamat, who bore all of them,
    𒈬𒌝𒈬mu𒌝mu 𒋾𒊩‍𒆳tiamat 𒈬𒀠𒇷𒁕𒀜mu𒀠𒇷𒁕at 𒁶𒊑𒋗𒌦gim𒊑𒋗un
  5. their waters as one they mingled:
    𒈨𒋗𒉡me𒋗nu ish𒋼nishish𒋼nish 𒄿𒄭𒆪𒈠i𒄭𒆪ma
  6. when reeds were not yet matted together, marshes had not yet appeared,
    𒄀𒉺𒊏gi𒉺ra 𒆷la 𒆠is𒋢𒊒kiis𒋢ru 𒋢𒊓𒀀su𒊓a 𒆷la she𒄿𒌋she𒄿u
  7. when the gods had not yet been fashioned, not one,
    𒂊𒉡𒈠e𒉡ma ilaniilani 𒆷la 𒋗𒁍𒌋shu𒁍u 𒈠𒈾𒈠ma𒈾ma
  8. none was called by name, destinies were not fixed:
    𒋗𒈠shuma 𒆷la zuk𒆪𒊒zuk𒆪ru 𒅆𒈠𒋫shi𒈠ta 𒆷la 𒅆𒄿𒈬shi𒄿mu
  9. then the gods were created in their midst.
    𒅁𒁀𒉡𒈠ib𒁀𒉡ma ilaniilani 𒆠𒆗𒋗𒌦ki𒆗𒋗un
  10. Lahmu and Lahamu were fashioned, were called by name;
    𒀭ilu 𒁻𒈬lahmu ilatilat 𒆷𒄩𒈬la𒄩mu 𒍑𒋫𒁍𒌋ush𒋫𒁍u 𒋗𒈪shumi 𒄑𒍠𒊒iz𒍠ru
  11. as they grew they became mighty.
    𒀀𒁲𒄿a𒁲i 𒅕𒁍𒌋ir𒁍u 𒄿𒅆hui𒅆hu
  12. Anshar and Kishar were createdthey were now more than they.
    𒀭sharanshar 𒀭ilu 𒆠sharkishar 𒅁𒁀𒉡𒌋ib𒁀𒉡u 𒂊𒇷𒋗𒉡e𒇷𒋗nu 𒀜𒊒atru
  13. Long were the days; years were added thereto:
    𒌨𒊑𒆪ur𒊑ku umeume us𒋛𒁍us𒋛pu shanateshanate
  14. Anu, their son, rival of his fathers
    𒀭ilu 𒀀𒉡𒌝a𒉡um 𒀀𒉈𒋗𒉡a𒉈𒋗nu sha𒎏shanin abe𒋗abeshu
  15. Anshar made Anu, his firstborn, their equal.
    𒀭sharanshar 𒀭ilu 𒀀𒉡𒌝a𒉡um 𒁍uk𒊏𒋗buuk𒊏shu 𒌋mashshil𒈠umashshilma
  16. Then Anu begat Nudimmud in his own image.
    𒌋u 𒀭ilu 𒀀𒉡𒌝a𒉡um 𒀭𒅆𒆷𒋗tam𒅆𒆷shu 𒌋lidulid 𒀭ilu 𒉡𒁴𒄷‍𒄭nu𒁴mud
  17. Nudimmud became master of his fathers;
    𒀭ilu 𒉡𒁴𒄷‍𒄭nu𒁴mud shasha abe𒋗abeshu shalit𒋗𒉡shalit𒋗nu 𒋗𒌋shuu
  18. keen openeared, thoughtful, mighty in strength,
    pal𒅗palka uz𒉌uzni 𒄩sishasis 𒂊𒈬kane𒈬kan 𒁍uk𒆪𒌌puuk𒆪ul
  19. stronger, by far, than his begetter, his father Anshar:
    𒄖𒍑shurgu𒍑shur 𒈠𒄿dishma𒄿dish 𒀀𒈾ana 𒀀litalit abi𒋗abishu 𒀭sharanshar
  20. he had no equal among the gods, his brothers.
    𒆷la 𒄿𒅆ishi sha𒎏shanin inaina ilaniilani 𒀜he𒂊𒋗athe𒂊shu
  21. So came into being the brothers, the gods.
    𒅔𒎏𒁺𒈠in𒎏𒁺ma 𒀜hu𒌋athuu ilaniilani 𒉌ni
  22. They perturbed Tiamat, they overpowered all of their guards,
    𒂊𒋗𒌋e𒋗u 𒋾𒊩‍𒆳tiamat kishatkishat 𒈾sir𒋗𒉡nasir𒋗nu ish𒋰𒁍ish𒋰bu
  23. They troubled the thoughts of Tiamat,
    𒁕𒀠hu𒉏𒈠da𒀠hu𒉏ma shasha 𒋾𒊩‍𒆳tiamat 𒋼‍𒀀as𒊓karassa
  24. With singing in the midst of Anduruna.
    𒄿𒈾ina 𒋗𒄿𒀀𒊒shu𒄿𒀀ru 𒋗𒁺𒊒shu𒁺ru 𒆠𒆗kirib 𒀭duru𒈾anduruna
  25. Apsu could not diminish their uproar,
    𒆷la 𒈾𒅆𒅕na𒅆ir 𒍪𒀊𒌋apsuu 𒊑𒁶𒋗𒌦ri𒁶𒋗un
  26. and Tiamat was distressed by their clamor;
    𒌋u 𒋾𒊩‍𒆳tiamat 𒋗𒅗𒄠𒈬𒈠𒀜shu𒅗𒄠𒈬𒈠at akkilakkil
  27. their deeds were obnoxious unto her,
    𒅎𒋻𒊓𒄠𒈠im𒋻𒊓𒄠ma ipshe𒋫𒋗𒌦ipshe𒋫𒋗un 𒂊𒇷shae𒇷sha
  28. their way was not good, for they had become powerful.
    𒆷la 𒋫battabat 𒀠kat𒋢𒉡alkat𒋢nu 𒋗𒉡𒋾shu𒉡ti 𒄿𒋫𒌀𒆷i𒋫𒌀la
  29. Then Apsu, the begetter of the great gods,
    𒄿𒉡𒋗i𒉡shu 𒍪𒀊apsu 𒍝𒊑zari ilaniilani 𒊏𒁉𒌋timra𒁉𒌋tim
  30. cried to Mummu, his messenger, saying unto him:
    is𒋛𒈠is𒋛ma 𒀭ilu 𒈬𒌝𒈬mu𒌝mu suk𒆗𒆷𒋗suk𒆗𒆷shu 𒄿𒍠𒋼‍𒀀𒋗i𒍠𒋼‍𒀀shu
  31. “Mummu, my messenger, who rejoicest my soul,
    𒀭ilu 𒈬𒌝𒈬mu𒌝mu suk𒆗𒇷suk𒆗li 𒈬tib𒁀mutibba 𒅗bit𒋾𒅀kabit𒋾ia
  32. come, unto Tiamat let us go.”
    𒀠𒄰𒈠al𒄰ma 𒋛𒊑ishsi𒊑ish 𒋾𒊩‍𒆳tiamat 𒄿i 𒉌𒅋likni𒅋lik
  33. They went and before Tiamat they sat down.
    𒅋𒇷𒆪𒈠il𒇷𒆪ma 𒆪𒌓𒈪ishku𒌓𒈪ish 𒋾𒊩‍𒆳tiamat sak𒁍sakpu
  34. They consulted on a plan concerning the gods, their sons.
    𒀀𒈠𒋾a𒈠ti 𒅎tal𒇷𒆪imtal𒇷ku ashshumashshum ilaniilani mare𒋗𒌦mare𒋗un
  35. Apsu opened his mouth, addressing her,
    𒍪𒀊apsu 𒉺𒀀𒋗pa𒀀shu 𒄿𒁍sham𒈠i𒁍shamma izakkar𒅆izakkarshi
  36. to the shining Tiamat he spoke:
    𒀀𒈾ana 𒋾𒊩‍𒆳tiamat 𒂖𒇷𒌅𒈠el𒇷𒌅ma 𒄿𒍠𒋼‍𒀀i𒍠kar 𒀀𒈠𒌈a𒈠tum
  37. “Their way annoys me.”
    𒅎𒊏asim𒊏as 𒀠kat𒋢𒉡alkat𒋢nu 𒂊𒇷𒅀e𒇷ia
  38. By day I am rested not, by night I sleep not.
    𒌨𒊏urra 𒆷la 𒋗up𒋗𒄩𒀝shuup𒋗𒄩ak 𒈬𒅆mushi 𒆷la 𒊓𒀠𒆷𒆪sa𒀠𒆷ku
  39. I will destroy them and confound their ways.
    𒇻𒍑hallik𒈠lu𒍑hallikma 𒀠kat𒋢𒉡alkat𒋢nu 𒇻shappiihlushappiih
  40. Let tranquillity reign, and let us sleep, even us.
    𒆪𒌋𒇻ku𒌋lu 𒇺shakin𒈠lishshakinma 𒄿 𒉌islalniislal 𒉌𒄿𒉡ni𒄿nu
  41. When Tiamat heard this,
    𒋾𒊩‍𒆳 𒀭𒉌𒋫an𒉌ta 𒄿𒈾ina she𒈨𒂊shashe𒈨𒂊sha
  42. She raged crying out to her husband.
    𒄿𒍪uz𒈠i𒍪uzma 𒅋𒋫𒋛il𒋫si 𒂊𒇷eli 𒄯𒈪shahar𒈪sha
  43. In pain she raged, she alone.
    mar𒋛ishmar𒋛ish ug𒄖gatug𒄖gat 𒂊dish𒅆shaedish𒅆sha
  44. She planned evil for herself:
    𒇷mut𒋫limutta 𒀉𒋫𒁲it𒋫di 𒀀𒈾ana 𒋼‍𒀀𒅆shakar𒅆sha
  45. How shall we destroy that which we have made?
    𒈪𒈾𒀀mi𒈾a 𒉌𒄿𒉡ni𒄿nu shasha 𒉌ip𒁍shamniip𒁍sham 𒉡𒍑hallaknu𒍑hallak
  46. Let their way be made troublesome but let us travel happily.
    𒀠kat𒋢𒉡alkat𒋢nu 𒇻lu shum𒊒𒊓𒈠shum𒊒𒊓ma 𒄿i 𒉌ish𒁺𒌓niish𒁺ud 𒋫bishtabish
  47. Mummu replied giving counsel to Apsu.
    𒄿𒁍𒌌𒈠i𒁍𒌌ma 𒀭ilu Mu𒌝𒈬Apsam 𒄿𒈠𒀠liki𒈠𒀠lik
  48. Wicked and not favorable was the advice of his ‘ Mummu ‘.
    rag𒄖raggu 𒌋u 𒆷la 𒈠𒄀𒊒ma𒄀ru 𒈪likmilik Mu𒌝𒈨𒋗
  49. Go, thou art able, even upon a gloomy way go,
    𒀀likalik 𒇷’𒀜 𒀠𒅗𒋫al𒅗ta 𒂊𒋛𒋫e𒋛ta
  50. Mayest thou have rest by day and by night mayest thou sleep.
    𒌨rishurrish 𒇻lu shup𒋗hatshup𒋗hat 𒈬shishmushish 𒇻lu 𒊩𒆷𒀜sal𒆷at
  51. Apsu hearkened unto him and his countenance brightened,
    ish𒈨shum𒈠ish𒈨shumma Apsu 𒅎𒈨𒊒im𒈨ru 𒉺𒉡𒍑𒋗pa𒉡𒍑shu
  52. At the injuries which he planned against the gods his sons.
    shasha 𒅆𒉌𒂊𒋾lim𒉌𒂊ti ik𒁍𒁺ik𒁍du 𒀀𒈾ana ilaniilani 𒈠𒊑𒂊𒋗ma𒊑𒂊shu
  53. The neck of Mummu he embraced.
    𒀭ilu Mu𒌝𒈬 𒄿𒋼diri𒋼dir 𒆠shad𒋢kishadsu
  54. He lifted him upon his knees as he kissed him.
    𒍑𒁀𒄠𒈠ush𒁀𒄠ma bir𒅗𒀀𒋗bir𒅗𒀀shu 𒌋𒈾sha𒆪u𒈾shaku sha𒀀𒋗sha𒀀shu
  55. Whatsoever they planned in their assembly,
    mim𒈬𒌋mim𒈬u ik𒁍𒁺ik𒁍du 𒁍uh𒊒𒍑shunpuuh𒊒𒍑shun
  56. Unto the gods their firstborn they repeated.
    𒀀𒈾ana ilaniilani 𒁍uk𒊑𒋗𒉡buuk𒊑𒋗nu 𒍑tan𒉡𒉌ushtan𒉡ni
  57. The gods wept as they hastened.
    id𒈬𒉏𒈠id𒈬𒉏ma ilaniilani 𒄿dul𒇻idullu
  58. Silence reigned and they sat whispering.
    𒆪𒇻kulu is𒁀𒌅is𒁀tu 𒊓𒆪𒌝𒈪issa𒆪𒌝𒈪is us𒁍usbu
  59. The exceedingly wise, the clever in skill,
    𒋗𒌉shutur uz𒉌uzni 𒀉pishaitpisha 𒋼𒇷’𒂊Ea,
  60. who knoweth all things, perceived their plan.
    𒀭ilu 𒂊𒀀 𒄩sishasis 𒈪𒅎𒈠𒈠mi𒅎𒈠ma 𒄿she’a 𒈨𒆠𒋗𒌦me𒆠𒋗un
  61. He devised for himself a curse having power over all things and he made it sure.
    𒅁shim𒈠ibshimma usratusrat 𒅗𒇷kali 𒌋kin𒋗ukinshu
  62. He made skillfully his pure incantation, surpassing all.
    𒌋nakkil𒋗unakkilshu 𒋗𒌅𒊒shu𒌅ru 𒋫𒀀𒋗ta𒀀shu 𒂖𒈝ellum
  63. He recited it and caused it to be upon the waters.
    𒅎𒉌shum𒈠im𒉌shumma inaina 𒈨me 𒌋shab𒅆ushabshi
  64. He bewitched him in sleep as he reposed in a cavern.
    shit𒀭shittam 𒅕𒋼𒄭𒋗ir𒋼𒄭shu 𒊓lilsalil 𒌅𒌒kit𒌈tu𒌒kittum
  65. Apsu he caused to slumber, bewitching the sleep.
    𒌋shaaslil𒈠ushaaslilma Apsa𒄠 𒊑𒄭rihi shit𒀭shittam
  66. Of Mummu whose manly parts frightfully he severed,
    𒀭ilu Mu𒌝𒈬 ut𒆷tushut𒆷tush 𒁕𒆷bishda𒆷bish 𒆪𒌋𒊒ku𒌋ru
  67. He severed his sinews and tore off his crown.
    ip𒌉iptur rik𒋛𒋗rik𒋛shu ish𒋫hatish𒋫hat 𒀀𒂵𒋗a𒂵shu
  68. His splendor he took from him, and he was dishonored.
    𒈨𒇴𒈨𒋗me𒇴𒈨shu 𒀉𒁀𒆷it𒁀la 𒋗𒌋shuu 𒌋𒋫𒁲iku𒋫𒁲ik
  69. Then he bound Apsu and slew him.
    ik𒈨𒋗𒈠ik𒈨𒋗ma Apsa𒄠 𒄿𒈾𒊏ash𒋗i𒈾𒊏ashshu
  70. Mummu he tied and his skull he crushed.
    𒀭ilu Mu𒌝𒈬 𒄿𒋫siri𒋫sir eli𒋗elishu ip𒋻𒅗ip𒋻ka
  71. He fixed upon Apsu his dwelling.
    𒌋kin𒈠ukinma elieli Apsi 𒋗bat𒋢shubatsu
  72. Mummu he seized and strengthened his bands.
    𒀭ilu Mu𒌝𒈬 𒀉𒋫mahit𒋫mah 𒌋danudan sirrit𒋢sirritsu
  73. After he had bound his enemies and had slain them,
    𒌌𒌅ultu 𒅆𒉌𒂊𒋗lim𒉌𒂊shu ik𒈬𒌋ik𒈬u 𒄿𒊓𒀀𒁺i𒊓𒀀du
  74. And he, Ea, had established his victory over his foes,
    𒀭ilu 𒂊𒀀 𒍑ziz𒍪ushzizzu 𒅕nit𒋫𒋗irnit𒋫shu elieli 𒂵𒊑𒋗ga𒊑shu
  75. And in his chamber he had become composed as one who is soothed,
    kirbishkirbish kum𒈪𒋗kum𒈪shu shup𒋗𒄭ishshup𒋗𒄭ish 𒄿𒉡uhhui𒉡uhhu
  76. He named it Apsu and they determined the holy places.
    𒅎𒁉shum𒈠im𒁉shumma Apsam 𒌋ad𒁺𒌋uad𒁺u esh𒊑𒂊𒋾esh𒊑𒂊ti
  77. Therein he caused to be founded his secret chamber.
    ash𒊒𒍑𒋗ash𒊒𒍑shu 𒄀pir𒊏𒋗gepir𒊏shu 𒌋sharshid𒈠usharshidma
  78. Lahmu and Lahamu his wife abode therein in majesty.
    𒀭ilu Lah𒈬 ilatilat La𒄩𒈬 𒄭𒊏tushhi𒊏tush inaina rab𒁀𒀀𒋼rab𒁀𒀀te 𒍑𒁍ushbu
  79. In the shrine of fates, the dwelling of concepts,
    inaina 𒆠is𒋛kiissi shimatishimati 𒀜𒈠𒀭at𒈠an usuratiusurati
  80. The wisest of the wise ones, the adviser of the gods, a god, was engendered.
    𒇷’𒌋𒇷’𒌋𒋾 abkalabkal ilaniilani 𒀭ilu 𒍑𒋻𒄭ush𒋻hi
  81. In the midst of the nether sea was born Ashur.
    inaina 𒆠𒆗kirib Apsi 𒅁𒁀𒉌ib𒁀ni 𒀭ilu Ashur
  82. In the midst of the pure nether sea was born Ashur.
    inaina 𒆠𒆗kirib ellielli Apsi 𒅁𒁀𒉌ib𒁀ni 𒀭ilu Ashur
  83. Lahmu his father begat him,
    𒅁𒉌𒋗𒈠ib𒉌𒋗ma 𒀭ilu Lah𒈬 𒀀𒁀𒋗a𒁀shu
  84. Lahamu his mother was his bearer.
    ilatilat La𒄩𒈬 𒄑‍𒆵𒋗ummashu 𒄯shaas𒋗harshaasshu
  85. He sucked at the breasts of goddesses.
    𒄿𒋾nik𒈠i𒋾nikma sirritsirrit Ishtarati
  86. A nurse tended him and filled him with terribleness.
    𒋫𒊑𒌅ta𒊑tu 𒀉𒋻𒊒𒋗it𒋻𒊒shu pul𒄩𒀀𒋫pul𒄩𒀀ta 𒍑𒈠𒀠𒇷ush𒈠𒀠li
  87. Enticing was his form, the gaze of his eye was brilliant.
    shamhatshamhat nabnit𒋢nabnitsu 𒊓𒊑𒅕sa𒊑ir 𒉌𒅆nishi 𒂊𒉌𒋗e𒉌shu
  88. Virile became his growth, he was given to procreation from the beginning.
    ut𒌅latut𒌅lat 𒋛𒋫𒋗si𒋫shu 𒈬shirmushir 𒌌𒌅ultu 𒌌𒆷ulla
  89. Lahmu, the begetter, his father beheld him.
    𒄿mur𒋗𒈠imur𒋗ma 𒀭ilu Lah𒈬 𒁀𒉡𒌋ba𒉡u abi𒋗abishu
  90. His heart rejoiced and was glad ; he was filled with joy.
    𒄿rishirish 𒅎mirimmir lib𒁀𒋗lib𒁀shu 𒄭𒁺𒋫hi𒁺ta 𒅎𒆷imla
  91. He perfected him and double godhead he added unto him.
    𒍑𒋼is𒁉shum𒈠ush𒋼is𒁉shumma 𒋗𒌦𒈾𒀜shu𒌦𒈾at iliili us𒋛ip𒋗us𒋛ipshu
  92. He was made exceedingly tall and he surpassed them somewhat.
    𒋗𒍑𒆪shu𒍑ku 𒈠’dish eli𒋗𒉡eli𒋗nu 𒀀𒋻atar mim𒈬𒈠mim𒈬ma
  93. Not comprehended were his measurements, and they were skillfully made.
    𒆷la 𒇴𒁕𒈠lam𒁕ma 𒉡uk𒆪𒆷nuuk𒆪la 𒈪𒈾𒌅𒋗mi𒈾𒌅shu
  94. They were not suited to be understood, and were oppressive to behold.
    𒄩𒊓𒋛ishha𒊓𒋛ish 𒆷la 𒈾𒋫𒀀na𒋫a 𒀀𒈠risha𒈠rish 𒉺ash𒅗paashka
  95. Four were his eyes, four were his ears.
    𒅕𒁀irba ena𒋗enashu 𒅕𒁀irba uzna𒋗uznashu
  96. When he moved his lips fire blazed forth.
